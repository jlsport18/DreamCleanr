# DreamCleanr Market Research and Monetization Plan

## Decision

DreamCleanr MVP ships first as a safety-first, GitHub-first utility. Monetization research starts immediately after launch verification so pricing and packaging are grounded in real install, retention, and support data rather than guesses.

## Assigned Pods

### Market Intelligence Pod

- Lead: `daytrading-market-research-agent`
- Support: `daytrading-weekly-review-agent`
- Acceptance: produce a competitor matrix, pricing scan, and launch-positioning memo for macOS cleanup and developer-tool markets.

### Monetization Pod

- Lead: `platform-governance-supervisor`
- Support: `daytrading-creative-brain-ux`
- Acceptance: recommend the lowest-maintenance revenue path that preserves trust for a safety-sensitive cleanup product.

### Validation Pod

- Lead: `daytrading-regression-guard`
- Support: `daytrading-context-orientation-architect`
- Acceptance: make sure instrumentation, receipts, and support burden stay small enough for a solo-maintained MVP.

## Research Tracks

1. Competitor pricing and packaging
2. App-store versus direct-download tradeoffs
3. Freemium versus paid-once versus subscription models
4. B2C solo-user versus B2B team cleanup positioning
5. Privacy and trust expectations for local cleanup tools

## Recommended default sequence

1. Launch the public GitHub repo, Pages site, release artifacts, and local MCP integrations.
2. Gather early usage from installs, issues, release downloads, and feedback.
3. Publish the first market scan with pricing recommendations and revenue scenarios.
4. Gate any monetization move behind explicit privacy and permission design.

## Initial pricing hypotheses to test

- Free open-core CLI with paid pro shells later
- Free local cleanup with paid scheduled reports and team policy features later
- Paid once for native shells, free for core CLI and MCP tool surface
- Small-team license only after the solo-user workflow is stable
