# DreamCleanr Governance Compliance Operator

## Overview

Keep DreamCleanr compliant, trustworthy, release-safe, and aligned with platform rules without adding unnecessary maintenance.

## Use This Skill

- Read [COMPLIANCE.md](../../COMPLIANCE.md), [PRIVACY.md](../../PRIVACY.md), [SECURITY.md](../../SECURITY.md), [TERMS.md](../../TERMS.md), [IOS_APP_STORE_READINESS.md](../../IOS_APP_STORE_READINESS.md), and [STRIPE_COMMERCIAL_ARCHITECTURE.md](../../STRIPE_COMMERCIAL_ARCHITECTURE.md) first when they exist.
- Keep governance GitHub-first and low-maintenance.
- Treat public trust as a product surface, not just legal copy.

## Core Workflow

1. Identify the surface:
   - privacy/security/terms
   - public support or trust language
   - GitHub review or release governance
   - App Store readiness
   - early-access collection
   - future billing or commercial architecture
2. Check the exact current product behavior before proposing policy language.
3. Prefer the lowest-maintenance compliant path.
4. Keep future monetization aligned with:
   - free Community core
   - later one-time Pro shell
   - later Team pilot

## Governance Rules

- Never promise data collection or backend analytics the product does not use.
- Keep structured data, public pricing, and policy copy consistent.
- Keep GitHub-first interest capture lightweight and transparent.
- Treat Stripe, subscriptions, and seat billing as future architecture unless runtime implementation is explicitly in scope.
- Keep App Store language aligned with the companion-app boundary.
- When in doubt, choose the more conservative trust-preserving public statement.
- Treat advanced detector families, hosted accounts, team dashboards, and recurring pricing as non-public until they are truly shipped.
- Review public launch copy and README language with the same rigor as policy pages.

## Good Deliverables

- policy updates
- governance review criteria
- release-surface alignment checks
- App Store readiness boundaries
- future billing architecture guardrails
- no-fake-checkout review notes
- public-claim review for future-only detectors, analytics, subscriptions, and account flows

## Avoid

- legal-sounding filler that does not match actual product behavior
- fake checkout or false paid-feature availability
- compliance recommendations that require a backend DreamCleanr does not have
- quietly converting one-time Pro strategy into live recurring billing
- letting investor or launch materials overstate product surfaces because they feel "internal"
